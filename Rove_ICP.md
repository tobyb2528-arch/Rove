# Rove — Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Brand:** Rove — gear for people who'd rather be outside.
**Category:** Outdoor / adventure / day-out gear (launch product: insulated backpack cooler).
**One-liner:** Well-made, good-looking gear that keeps up with you and makes getting outside easier.

---

## Primary ICP — "The Weekend Adventurer"

**Snapshot:** Sarah, 32. Lives in a mid-sized town or city suburb, works a full-time professional job (marketing, teaching, healthcare, tech). Spends the week at a desk and lives for the weekend — hikes, beach trips, camping, festivals, park hangs with friends. Not a hardcore mountaineer; a normal person who genuinely prioritises getting outside.

### Demographics
- Age 27–40 (core), skewing 28–35
- Household income £30k–£60k (comfortable, discretionary spend on lifestyle gear)
- Urban/suburban, often with a car
- Mix of couples, young families, and friend groups
- UK primary; extends to any temperate-climate market

### Psychographics — what makes them tick
- Values experiences over things, but will happily pay for *things that enable experiences*
- Aspires to an outdoorsy, active identity — the "adventure" version of themselves
- Cares how their gear looks (it's part of the aesthetic, shows up on their Instagram)
- Time-poor, so values gear that's easy, reliable, and low-hassle
- Willing to pay a bit more to avoid cheap tat that breaks

### Goals — what they want
- Make days out easier and more enjoyable (cold drinks, less faff, hands free)
- Look good doing it — gear that fits their aesthetic
- Buy once, buy well — not replace a cheap version next summer

### Pain points / frustrations
- Warm drinks and soggy, leaking cool bags ruining a day out
- Cheap coolers that break, smell, or look ugly
- Juggling a cooler + bag + kids/dog with no free hands
- Generic, unbranded gear that feels disposable

### Buying triggers
- Start of summer / first warm weekend
- Booking a festival, camping trip, or holiday
- Seeing the product used well on TikTok/Instagram
- Upgrading from a cheap cooler that let them down

### Where they are (for marketing)
- Instagram and TikTok (outdoor, travel, "day in my life" content)
- Pinterest (trip planning, packing lists)
- Facebook groups for local walks, camping, festivals

### Price sensitivity
Mid — won't buy the cheapest, won't overpay for hype. £35–45 feels "quality but reasonable." **This is exactly where Rove should sit.**

---

## Secondary ICP — "The Practical Family Organiser"

Parents, 30–45, who run the logistics of family days out. They're buying the cooler to make beach days, picnics, and park trips smoother with kids. They value capacity, durability, easy cleaning, and leakproofing above aesthetics (though they still want it to look decent). Higher lifetime value — they'll come back for more day-out gear. Reach them on Facebook and Instagram, triggered by school holidays and summer.

---

## Secondary ICP — "The Festival & Social Crowd"

18–28, buying for festivals, park drinking, beach hangs with friends. More price-sensitive and impulse-driven, heavily TikTok-led. They care that it looks cool and holds a lot (the 30-can capacity and bottle opener are big selling points here). Lower margins but high volume and strong viral potential — great for TikTok content even if they're not your core profit driver.

---

## How to use this

- **Build all your messaging around the Weekend Adventurer** — that's where the healthy margins and brand fit are. Copy, photography, and ads should speak to that "rather be outside" identity.
- **Lead marketing on TikTok/Instagram** with real-use lifestyle content (cooler at the beach, top of a hike, at a campsite).
- **Anchor pricing at £39–42** — squarely in the Weekend Adventurer's "quality but fair" zone, above the disposable-cooler crowd.
- **Let the secondary segments guide seasonal pushes** — families at school holidays, festival crowd at festival season — without diluting the core brand.
